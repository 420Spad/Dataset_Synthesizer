6 Problem Space
7 -------------
8 Research implementations of DL (Deep Learning) typically couple simulation with neural network training, creating a 1:1 relationship that's difficult to iterate and scale. Modifications to the parameter space commonly force recompilation and redeployment of both the simulation and the neural network. Also, this misses opportunities to parallelize the training of multiple networks / kernels. For example, multiple variations of a neural network could be trained concurrently to more quickly determine the optimal algorithm.
9 
10 Additionally, multiple teams are replicating work to setup common framework and pipeline tooling. Another example of this is the generation of synthetic data to augment datasets used for neural network training. Here, the DLToolkit provides configurable support to generate, transform, and reuse virtual environment variations (which itself could be horizontally scaled), and format conversion support to provide such content across incompatible frameworks used by different teams.
11 
12 The DLToolkit addresses these issues to maximize the effectiveness of DL research and development while enabling teams the flexibility to continue using frameworks they've already invested in, e.g. TensorFlow, Caffe, Pytorch, not limited to Keras etc.

17 Goals
18 -----
19 1. Decouple Simulation and Machine Learning
20  - Support communication between simulations (typically native) and Neural Networks (typically python).
21  - Simulation provides neural network input remotely.
22  - Connected neural networks being trained return actions to simulation controllers.
23  - Cross-platform communication supported between simulations and neural networks via sockets.
24 
25 2. Connect Simulations and Networks in Configurable Manner, supporting rapid iteration
26  - Allow for on the fly neural network modifications (via config JSON) without simulation code iteration.
27  - Support multiple algorithmic learning approaches without extensive change to simulations.
28 
29 3. Scale Learning Problems Across Multiple Simulation Instances and Networks
30  - Ingest data from multiple simulation instances simultaneously.
31  - Route data to one or more neural networks being trained or tested simultaneously.
32  - NxM simulation instance to network instance relationship, scaled independently.
33  - Facilitate deployment within one machine or horizontally scaled across several machines.
34 
35 4. Synthetic Data Generation at scale
36  - Generation, transformation, and reuse of virtual environment variations to support neural network training.
37  - Content format conversion support between incompatible frameworks.
38  - Facilitate data tagging, query, and storage/distribution for production / consumption at scale.
